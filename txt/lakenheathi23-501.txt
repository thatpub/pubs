This instruction implements AFPD 23-5, Reusing and Disposing of Materiel. It establishes procedures
applicable to the Precious Metal Recovery Program (PMRP). It applies to all units assigned to the 48th
Fighter Wing that generate precious metals. Ensure all records created as a result of processes prescribed
in this publication are maintained in accordance with AFMAN 37-123, Management of Records, and dis-
posed of in accordance with Air Force Records Disposition Schedule located at https://
afrims.amc.af.mil.

1. Program Responsibilities.
   1.1. The Mission Support Group Commander (MSG/CC) will appoint the Logistics Readiness Squad-
   ron, Materiel Management Flight, Inspection Section NCOIC as the Installation PMRP monitor.
   1.2. The Installation PMRP monitor will:

    1.2.1. Ensure the Precious Metal Indicator Listing (L10) is generated at least quarterly to produce
    a listing identifying those National Stock Numbers (NSN) against which a precious metal indica-
    tor is assigned.
    1.2.2. Distribute a copy of the above list to each agency identified as producing Precious Metals
    (PM), with a cover letter to be returned to 48th Logistics Readiness Section Inspection Element
    (48 LRS/LGRMQ). This letter will be filed in the PMRP jacket file.
    1.2.3. Conduct a semi-annual survey containing specific monthly criteria, as determined by the
    Installation PMRP monitor, for all generating activities.
    1.2.4. Provide adequate training to all PM generating agency monitors on at least a semi-annual
    basis.
    1.2.5. Advertise the PMRP through articles in base publications, online, or at unit commanders'
    calls or other media outlets as required.
    1.2.6. Prepare Department of Defense (DD) Form 1348-1A Part Two, Single Line Item Release/
    Receipt Document, for all material received. The DD Form 1348-1A will contain the following:
       1.2.6.1. Quantity and unit of issue.
       1.2.6.2. Document Number (assigned from the inspection log).
       1.2.6.3. Signature and grade of the Installation Monitor.
       1.2.6.4. Nomenclature.
       1.2.6.5. Person who turned in material.
       1.2.6.6. Demilitarization code.
       1.2.6.7. Condition code.
       1.2.6.8. Standard Waste and Scrap Classification code list.
       1.2.6.9. Base PMRP monitor's name.
    1.2.7. Ensure activities generating PM bearing materials appoint a unit primary and alternate
    PMRP monitor who will establish jacket file accounts and manage the unit program in accordance
    with this instruction, AFMAN 23-110 Volume 6 Chapter 4; Precious Metals Recovery Program,
    AFI 31-101, The Air Force Installation Security Program, and DOD 4160.21-M, Defense Mate-
    rial Disposition Management.
    1.2.8. Ensure a copy of the designated Installation PMRP is sent to all PM generating activities to
    be placed in their PM jacket file.
    1.2.9. Ensure each activity in the PMRP initiates and maintains a self-inspection program. This
    program will include as a minimum AFI 31-101 requirements, to include the periodic testing of
    hypo solution draining from electrolytic cartridge units, to verify that units are in compliance with
    receipts, issue, and turn-in requirements.
    1.2.10. Maintain liaison with the Defense Reutilization and Marketing Service (DRMS) and Pre-
    cious Metals Area Representative (PMAR) to keep abreast of PM recovery techniques and to
    obtain or give assistance on the overall PMRP.

  1.3. The installation PMRP monitor assigned to 48 LRS and 48 MDSS, Medical Logistics Section
  will:
     1.3.1. Maintain a file of all accountable transactions applicable to the receipt and transfer of PM.
     1.3.2. Provide secure storage for PM awaiting disposition according to AFI 31-101.
     1.3.3. At least quarterly, transfer all on-hand PM to the DRMO facility at RAF Molesworth.
     1.3.4. Provide a quarterly report of all PM turned in to the DRMO facility at RAF Molesworth to
     48 LRS/LGRS (Management and Systems Flight). NOTE: 48 MDSS/SGSL (Medical Logistics)
     will also send a copy to the installation monitor.
     1.3.5. Be familiar with the contents of this instruction, AFMAN 23-110 Volume 6 Chapter 4, AFI
     31-101, and DOD Manual 4160.21-M.
  1.4. Each agency generating PM will:
     1.4.1. Appoint, in writing, a primary and alternate PMRP monitor to 48 LRS/LGRMQ. This letter
     will include Rank, Name, DEROS, Building, Duty Phone, Squadron Office Symbol, and, as appli-
     cable, type of recovery equipment used, type of precious metal scrap generated and the type of
     fine and high precious metals content used such as anodes, brazing and solder which will have a
     controlled item code (CIC) R assigned.
         1.4.1.1. Be familiar with the contents of this instruction, AFMAN 23-110 Volume 6 Chapter
         4, AFI 31-101, and DOD Manual 4160.21-M.
     1.4.2. Be responsible for the harvesting of precious metals and subsequent turn-in to 48 LRS/
     LGRMQ.
     1.4.3. All jacket file accounting systems will include the following: NOTE: All surveys, reviews,
     inspection results, reports and letters will be maintained in the jacket folder for a period of one
     year.
         1.4.3.1. TAB A:
            1.4.3.1.1. Current letter assigning primary and alternate PMRP monitors.
            1.4.3.1.2. Current copy of message or letter identifying primary and alternate installation
            PMRP monitors.
         1.4.3.2. TAB B:
            1.4.3.2.1. Lakenheath Instruction 23-501, Precious Metal Recovery Program.
            1.4.3.2.2. AFMAN 23-110 Volume 6 Chapter 4.
            1.4.3.2.3. DODM 4160.21-M.
            1.4.3.2.4. AFI 31-101, to include all changes and supplements.
            1.4.3.2.5. Precious Metal Listing (L10) available at https://48lrs-s1/supweb/PMRP/
            pmrp.htm.
            1.4.3.2.6. Using organization’s operational instructions to include:
                1.4.3.2.6.1. Procedures for using and maintaining organizational precious metal recov-
                ery unit.

               1.4.3.2.6.2. Procedures for turn-ins or shipments of harvested PM. NOTE: If publi-
               cations are not contained in this TAB, establish an Optional Form 21, Cross-Refer-
               ence, identifying location of publication. (Exception: Lakenheath Instruction 23-501

    1.4.4. Provide a secure storage area for all PM bearing material at all times until transferred to the
    installation PMRP monitor. The PM material will be secured if not under surveillance.
    1.4.5. Maintain and properly secure any electrolytic recovery unit(s) (ERU) issued by the Defense
    Property Disposal Region Europe.
    1.4.6. Appoint in writing separate individuals who will:
       1.4.6.1. Receipt for issue and turn-in to the DRMO line PM and PM bearing items assigned
       control item code "R".
       1.4.6.2. Harvest silver flakes and sludge from electrolytic units and remove and replace recov-
       ery cartridges.
       1.4.6.3. Not be involved in the PMRP to witness the harvesting, cartridge removal, and weight
       certification.

        1.4.6.4. Receipt for issue and turn-in fine precious metals bearing items assigned controlled
        item code "R" in areas of operations such as plating, grinding, boring, and stripping.
     1.4.7. Submit a letter to the Installation PMRP monitor for all PM turned in each quarter. This
     will be done not later than the 5th duty day of the first month of the next quarter.
     1.4.8. The unit PMRP monitors will include the PMRP in their self-inspection program. As a
     minimum include AFI 31-101 requirements, periodic testing of hypo solution draining from elec-
     trolytic cartridge units to ensure units are operating properly, if applicable, and are in compliance
     with receipt, issue and turn-in requirements.
     1.4.9. The unit PMRP monitor must schedule the turn-in of PM materials with the Installation
     PMRP monitor. The PM material must be weighed prior to turn-in.
     1.4.10. If both primary and alternate unit PMRP monitors are TDY, on leave, or sick at the same
     time for more than a week, then an alternate will be assigned and the Installation PMRP monitor
     must be notified.
     1.4.11. The unit PMRP monitor must brief all personnel in all generating activities on the PMRP
     and its procedures. As a minimum, the brief will cover the procedures identifying PM and the
     turn-in of PM to the unit's PMRP monitor.
     1.4.12. The unit PMRP monitor will ensure preventative maintenance on silver recovery units is
     completed as follows:
        1.4.12.1. Day-to-day adjustments.
        1.4.12.2. Cleaning.
        1.4.12.3. Replacement of fuses, gaskets, and hoses.
        1.4.12.4. The unit PMRP monitor will conduct weekly tests of the electrolytic silver recovery
        unit and silver recovery cartridge with silver estimating strips. The test results and who per-
        formed the test will be annotated and filed in TAB E of the PMRP jacket file. Results of the
        reading should not be higher than 1.75. If they exceed this limit, the unit must be cleaned or
        changed immediately.
        1.4.12.5. The unit PMRP monitor will establish a system to identify how much fixer has gone
        through the electrolytic or silver recovery cartridge unit. The unit will maintain a log of this
        quantity.
