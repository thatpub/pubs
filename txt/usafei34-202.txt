This instruction implements AFPD 34-1, Air Force Services Combat Support Programs. Supports
policy according to AFI 34-270, Air Force Library and Information System (AFLIS). It states mission,
policies, organization, administration, operation and implementation procedures of the HQ USAFE
Library Service Center (LSC) for contingency and deployment, branch, field, site libraries; and office
collections of the United States Air Forces in Europe (USAFE). It applies to all Department of Defense
(DoD) activities for which USAFE has library support responsibility. This instruction does not apply to
Air National Guard (ANG) or Air Force Reserve Command (AFRC) units. It prescribes USAFE Form
245, Application for Library Service. Ensure that all records created as a result of processes prescribed
in this publication are maintained in accordance with Air Force Manual (AFMAN) 33-363, Management
of Records, and disposed of in accordance with the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (RDS)
located     in     Air    Force     Records     Information     Management        System      (AFRIMS):
https://www.my.af.mil/gcss-af61a/afrims/afrims/rims.cfm. This instruction requires collecting and
maintaining information protected by the Privacy Act of 1974 authorized by 10, U.S.C., 8013.

SUMMARY OF CHANGES

This Interim Change (IC-2) clarifies branch libraries staffing and revises responsibilities of who has
oversight of the Library Service Center. It also updates the office symbol references. Revisions are
indicated by a vertical bar (|) in the left margin.
1. Program Overview. The LSC directs, implements, and ensures support of world-wide mission,
education, and quality of life information requirements and promotes the productive use of leisure time,
as applicable, to the following:
   1.1. Geographically Separated Units (GSU).
   1.2. Contingency and Deployment Operations.

2. Library Service Center. AFI 34-270 authorizes extension services and specialized assistance be
provided to personnel without access to established library and information services.
     2.1. Branch Libraries. Administered and serviced by the LSC, branch libraries offer a wide range of
     materials and services. These libraries are separate facilities, with set operating hours, paid
     Appropriated Funds (APF) staff, and a basic collection of materials on receipt from the LSC.
     2.2. Contingency and Deployment Library Support. Administered and serviced by LSC,
     contingency and deployment support provides non-accountable paperback and periodical kits.
     2.3. Field Libraries. Field libraries contain a collection of general library materials issued on a
     custody receipt to an Air Force, DoD, or State Department activity for which the Air Force has
     support responsibility (see DoD Instruction (DoDI) 1015.10, Programs for Military Morale,
     Welfare, and Recreation (MWR), Enclosure 11 ,with associated Memorandum, Policy Revision for
     DoD Morale, Welfare and Recreation (MWR) Support to Military Missions in Foreign Countries,
     dated 19 Dec 01 and DoD Directive (DoDD) 5100.3, Support of the Headquarters of Combatant
     and Subordinate Joint Commands). Administered and serviced by the LSC, field libraries will
     receive paperback and periodical kits and materials from rotating collections only. Field libraries
     may or may not have set operating hours or paid staff. They may operate on the honor system.
     Unassigned components may be supported with excess materials after all other LSC obligations have
     been met.
     2.4. Site Libraries. Administered and serviced by the LSC, site libraries are collections of popular
     hardbacks, paperback books, newspapers, periodicals and audiovisual materials issued to library-
     inaccessible locations. They may or may not have set operating hours or paid staff. They may
     operate on the honor system.
     2.5. International Military Activities. These are North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO) units
     designated in DoD 7000.14-R, Volume 11A, Reimbursable Operations, Policy and Procedures,
     Chapter 9, which receive no other library support. Units may receive materials from the rotating
     collections, and paperback and periodical kits only.
     2.6. Geographically Separated Unit libraries (paragraphs 2.1. through 2.5.) are categorized as:
        2.6.1. Branch library-100 to 1,000 assigned US military (paragraphs 2.1. through 2.5.).
        2.6.2. Field Library-20 to 500 assigned US military (paragraphs 2.1. through 2.5.).
        2.6.3. Site library-1 to 200 assigned US military (paragraphs 2.1. through 2.5.).
        2.6.4. Unit not tenant on a DoD installation.
        2.6.5. Unit without ready access to a DoD library.
     2.7. Office Reference. Collections are accountable publications and periodical subscriptions issued
     by custody receipt from the LSC to an activity for direct mission support, and subject to annual
     inventory.
     2.8. Reimbursable Support. Reimbursable support units are non-Air-Force activities that have a
     signed Memorandum of Agreement (MOA) for library and information materials and service on a
     reimbursable basis (DoDI 4000.19, Interservice and Intragovernmental Support, Enclosure 2,
     Paragraph E2.1.10).

   2.9. Materials Provided. As applicable, select, order, process and ship materials to the field

3. Responsibilities . This instruction establishes the following responsibilities and authorities for the
LSC and the activities served.
   3.1. The HQ USAFE Manpower, Personnel, and Services Directorate, Personnel Support Division,
   Education Operations Branch Chief (HQ USAFE/A1KE) will oversee the LSC.
   3.2. The HQ USAFE LSC Director will direct, develop, implement, and administer the HQ USAFE
   Library Service Center Program.
       3.2.1. Determine, verify and initiate services.
       3.2.2. Prepare budgets and manage financial resources.
       3.2.3. Liaison with a wide range of federal departments and agencies.
       3.2.4. Direct selection, ordering, processing, document and control procedures, automation
       projects, inventories, receipt, and redistribution from closing base libraries as well as from LSC
       branch, site, and field libraries.
       3.2.5. Provide training and management assistance to LSC activities.
       3.2.6. Provide reference research and analysis to include on-line and database searches.

       3.4.1. DoD components must request services in writing. If the request is in accordance with
       DoDI 1015.10, as amended by Memorandum signed 19 Dec 01, locations designated to receive
       AF support must forward a USAFE Form 245 to the LSC. Upon receipt of a signed USAFE
       Form 245, LSC will initiate services. Unassigned DoD components may be supported
       (paragraph 2.3.). Send requests from DoD components not assigned Air Force support to the
       designated supporting branch.
       3.4.2. DoD-designated coordinator will ensure timely rotation of materials per LSC instructions.
       3.4.3. Accountable materials are subject to inventory and reimbursement.
    3.5. Contingency and Deployment Responsibilities.
       3.5.1. In accordance with AFI 34-270, paragraph A3.8.6.1., the USAFE LSC will provide
       library support for short term emergency outside the continental United States (OCONUS)
       contingency operations, deployments, and remote sites. The owning major command
       (MAJCOM) will identify to the Library Service Center operations requiring support. For support
       requiring reimbursement (for sites other than USAFE sites), the owning MAJCOM will
       reimburse USAFE through central office funds, notifying Air Force Services Agency/Libraries
       Branch (HQ AFSVA/SVXL) in writing of total amounts required for reimbursement.
       3.5.2. Taskings for support must be routed through the chains of command for official approval.
       3.5.3. Owning MAJCOMs will establish a coordinator (name, unit, address, fax, e-mail, if
       available) forwarding the information to the HQ USAFE Manpower, Personnel, and Services
       Directorate, Personnel Support Division, Education Operations Branch Chief (HQ
       USAFE/A1KE) and the LSC Director.
       3.5.4. The LSC will support these operations with non-accountable paperback book and
       periodical kits.
