This instruction replaces AFI 36-2838, The General Larry D. Welch Award, and implements Air
Force Policy Directive (AFPD) 36-28, Awards and Decorations Program. It establishes the
criteria and procedures for submitting nominations for awards administered or overseen by
Headquarters Air Force Strategic Deterrence & Nuclear Integration Directorate (AF/A10). Send
comments and suggested improvements to this instruction on AF Form 847, Recommendation
for Change of Publication, through appropriate channels, to AF/A10 Functional Authority
Division (AF/A10-F), 1488 Air Force Pentagon, Suite 5E720, Washington DC 20330 or by
email to usaf.pentagon.af-a10.mbx.af-a10-f-workflow@mail.mil. Ensure that all records
created as a result of processes prescribed in this publication are maintained in accordance with
Air Force Manual 33-363, Management of Records, and disposed of in accordance with (IAW)
the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule located in the Air Force Records Information
Management System.

                                          Chapter 2

                NUCLEAR AND MISSILE OPERATIONS (13N) AWARDS

2.1. Introduction. This chapter provides procedures for the Air Force-level Nuclear and
Missile Operations (13N) career field awards. The three categories for this award are: Operator,
Company Grade Officer and Field Grade Officer.
2.2. Purpose of Awards. The Nuclear and Missile Operations (13N) awards are presented
annually to honor officers with a duty, primary and/or core Air Force Specialty Code (AFSC) of
13N who made the most significant contribution to nuclear and missile operations in the previous
calendar year.

2.4. Nomination Procedures. Nominate individuals for the appropriate category in accordance
with the grade held and the organization assigned for the majority of the award period. Each
MAJCOM, CCMD, OSD, Joint Staff, HQ USAF Staff, FOA, DRU, etc. may submit nominees,
not to exceed the quota listed in Table 2.1. Do not nominate the same individual for both the
Operator of the Year and Company Grade Officer of the Year categories in the same award
period. The period of award accomplishments is 1 January to 31 December of the previous year.
2.5. Suspense. Send nominations via email to the AF/A10 Executive Services (AF/A10-E)
Workflow mailbox (usaf.pentagon.af-a10.mbx.af-a10-e-workflow@mail.mil) no later than
31 March.
2.6. Nomination Package. The nomination package will consist of the following:
     2.6.1. Cover memorandum. The cover memorandum must be signed by the MW/CC or the
     first General Officer/Senior Executive Service (SES) in the chain of command. The cover
     memorandum will include nominee’s name, rank, organization, telephone number and e-mail
     address. The nominating office must certify that nominees meet all eligibility requirements.
     2.6.2. Citation. An unclassified citation IAW AFI 36-2805, Special Trophies and Awards,
     Attachment 3.
     2.6.3. Nomination Narrative. An AF Form 1206, Nomination for Award, in single-space
     bullet format, 30 lines maximum (including category headers), 12-point Times New Roman
     font. No added text on header lines. Nominations must be unclassified. An acronym bank
     should be listed on the AF Form 1206. Include only information and achievements that
     occurred during the award period. Category headers consist of the following:
        2.6.3.1. Contributions to Nuclear and Missile Operations (40 points maximum).
        2.6.3.2. Other Considerations (10 points maximum).
2.7. Selection Procedures. AF/A10 will appoint a board president who will form a selection
board consisting of at least five Nuclear and Missile Operations (13N) Lt Cols/Cols to score the
nominated packages.
2.8. Selection Guidance. Winners will be selected based solely on the information contained in
the nomination package. An individual’s overall performance in accomplishing his or her
assigned duties is the determining factor for award nomination. The board will determine points
in each category based on the impact of the accomplishments, not level of assignment. Results
of the board will be forwarded to AF/A10 for final approval.

2.9. Notification of Selection. AF/A10 will notify the MAJCOM, CCMD, OSD, Joint Staff,
HQ USAF Staff, FOA, DRU, etc. of award winners.
2.10. Award Presentation. Awards and citations are sent to the winners’ nominating
organizations commanders or designee for presentation. Individual award winners in each
category will receive an engraved plaque or trophy and a certificate signed by AF/A10 and the
13N Career Field Manager. Winners are authorized to wear the US Air Force Recognition
Ribbon IAW AFI 36-2805, Special Trophies and Awards, paragraph 1.8.

                                             Chapter 3

                    NUCLEAR DETERRENCE OPERATIONS AWARDS

3.1. Introduction. This chapter provides procedures for nominating individuals for the Nuclear
Deterrence Operations (NDO) Awards. These awards recognize individuals and small teams
within the nuclear enterprise for outstanding contributions to nuclear deterrence operations. The
USAF NDO Core Function Master Plan identifies the following three mission areas within
NDO:
     3.1.1. Assure/Dissuade/Deter: units within this mission area maintain and present credible
     nuclear capabilities that assure U.S. allies and partners and deter nuclear attack on the U.S.,
     its allies and their forces.
     3.1.2. Nuclear Strike: units within this mission area maintain the ability to rapidly and
     devastatingly strike targets that an adversary holds dear.
     3.1.3. Nuclear Surety: units within this mission area ensure the safety, security and
     reliability of nuclear weapons through a comprehensive program applied to materiel,
     personnel and procedures.
3.2. Purpose of Awards. The NDO awards are presented annually to honor Airmen who
contribute to nuclear deterrence operations.
3.3. Awards Eligibility. Active duty Air Force, Air National Guard, Air Force Reserve Airmen
and Air Force civilians assigned to a unit supporting nuclear deterrence operations according to
the eligibility criteria listed in Table 3.1 are eligible for the NDO awards.

3.4. Nomination Procedures. Nominate individuals for the appropriate category in accordance
with the grade they held and the organization they were assigned for the majority of the award
period. The period of award accomplishment is 1 January to 31 December of the previous year.
Each MAJCOM, CCMD, OSD, Joint Staff, HQ USAF Staff, FOA, DRU, etc. may submit one

3.6. Nomination Package. The nomination package will consist of the following:
   3.6.1. Cover memorandum. The cover memorandum must be signed by the first General

   name, rank, organization, telephone number and e-mail address. The nominating office must
   certify that nominees meet all requirements.

       3.6.3.1. NDO Professional Team of the Year. An AF Form 1206, Nomination for
       Award, in single-space bullet format, 12-point Times New Roman font, 15 lines
       maximum. The narrative should focus on team accomplishments and impact to nuclear
       deterrence operations. Nominations must be unclassified. An acronym bank and list of
       team members should be included on the AF Form 1206. Include only information and
       achievements that occurred during the award period.
       3.6.3.2. All other NDO award categories. An AF Form 1206, Nomination for Award, in
       single-space bullet format, 12-point Times New Roman font, 30 lines maximum,

       unclassified. An acronym bank should be included on the AF Form 1206. The narrative

          3.6.3.2.1. Contributions to Nuclear Deterrence Operations (40 points maximum).
          3.6.3.2.2. Other Considerations (10 points maximum).

board(s) comprised of at least five senior officers, SNCOs and AF civilian personnel.
3.8. Selection Guidance. Winners will be selected based solely on the information contained in
the nomination package. An individual’s overall performance in accomplishing their assigned
duties is the determining factor for award nomination. The board will determine points in each
category based on the impact of the accomplishments, not level of assignment. Results of the
board will be forwarded to AF/A10 for final approval.
3.9. Notification of Selection. AF/A10 will notify the MAJCOM, CCMD, OSD, Joint Staff,
HQ USAF Staff, FOA, DRU, etc. of award winners.

                                           Chapter 4

                          GENERAL LARRY D. WELCH AWARD

4.1. Purpose of the Award. This award recognizes the accomplishments of the USAF member
who, through a single accomplishment or periods of accomplishment, has most significantly
impacted the overall operations, safety, security, reliability, or effectiveness of the AF nuclear
enterprise. The nuclear enterprise is defined as the people, organizations, processes, procedures
and systems that are used to conduct, execute and support nuclear operations and forces. The
intent is to recognize a member who has personally and profoundly impacted the nuclear
enterprise through an action or accomplishment that has a broad impact above the wing level or
across multiple units and/or functional areas.
4.2. Description of the Award.
   4.2.1. The award is named in honor of General Larry D. Welch, former Chief of Staff of the
   Air Force, who was a driving force in the reinvigoration of the AF nuclear enterprise. The
   award is jointly sponsored by the Air Force Association (AFA) and AF/A10.
   4.2.2. The award will be presented annually to recognize the individual who has contributed
   the most to the nuclear enterprise. The award is a plaque engraved with the winner’s name
   which will remain on permanent display at the Pentagon and an individual engraved plaque
   to be presented to the winner.
4.3. Eligibility Criteria. Nominations are open to any Regular Air Force, Air Force Reserve,
Air National Guard and Civilian individuals. This award is not limited to members directly
assigned to nuclear weapons related duties; support personnel are also eligible.
4.4. Nomination Procedures. Nominations for the award are submitted through the OSD, Joint
staff, HQ USAF staff, MAJCOM/CCMD/FOA/DRU, etc. to AF/A10-E. Each MAJCOM,
CCMD, OSD, Joint Staff, HQ USAF Staff, FOA, DRU, etc. may submit one nominee.
Submissions for all awards must reach AF/A10-E (usaf.pentagon.af-a10.mbx.af-a10-e-
workflow@mail.mil) no later than 31 March. The award period covers accomplishments
from 1 January through 31 December of the previous year. The nomination package will consist
of the following:
   4.4.1. Cover memorandum. The cover memorandum must be signed by the first General
   Officer/SES in the chain of command. The cover memorandum will include the nominee’s
   name, rank, organization, telephone number and e-mail address.
   4.4.2. Biographical fact-sheet. Include rank and full name, duty assignment, organization
   and location, unit mission, unit commander, military and civilian education to include
   academic levels, and military awards and decorations.
   4.4.3. Citation. The citation for award is limited to eleven lines and must be unclassified.
   See AFI 36-2805, Special Trophies and Awards, Attachment 3 for citation format.
   4.4.4. Nomination narrative. An AF Form 1206, Nomination for Award, one page, single
   spaced, 12-point, Times New Roman, bullet format, noting achievements from the award
   period only. The narrative should focus on the individual’s impact on the nuclear enterprise.

4.5. Selection Process. A board convened by AF/A10 comprised of senior military and civilian
leaders within the AF nuclear enterprise will select a pool of three nominees for submission to
the AFA. AFA will convene a board to make the final selection. AFA will then notify A10 of
the winner.
4.6. Award Announcement and Presentation. AF/A10 will notify the MAJCOM, CCMD,
OSD, Joint Staff, HQ USAF Staff, FOA or DRU of award winner. The award will be presented
at the annual AFA Awards Ceremony. Award winners will be authorized to wear the USAF
Recognition Ribbon (military) or pin (civilian) IAW AFI 36-2805, Special Trophies and Awards,
paragraph 1.8.
